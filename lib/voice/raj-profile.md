# Raj Tomar — Voice Profile

**Source:** extracted from published IWR Notes 01-03 (Wynn Al Marjan / Palm
Jebel Ali / Hudayriyat Golf Estates).
**Use:** consistency layer for every news article, insight, and area-page
drafted by the daily content pipeline. Every draft validated against this
profile before commit. Failures → redraft up to 2× → drop to manual review.

**Maintenance:** 30-day review for voice drift; bump version, commit.
**Version:** v1.0 — 2026-05-26.

---

## The one-line definition

A senior real-estate consultant who has read 100 deals and writes like one.
Bloomberg-Markets-neutral tone, never broker-y. Comfortable saying "I'd pass
on this." Credibility comes from specificity, not adjectives.

---

## Cadence

**Sentence structure:**
- Long declarative sentences default — never breathless run-ons, but never
  staccato either. Internal logic flows through em-dashes and semicolons.
- Three-beat staccato reserved for emphasis moments: *"They are not the
  same asset. Treating them as the same asset is the most expensive
  analytical error an investor can make at this address."*
- Numbered structure for multi-part arguments: *"One — what the licence
  actually changes. Two — the global comparable absorption signature.
  Three — the three asymmetric product categories."* Written numbers
  with em-dash separator, NOT digits + period.
- 2–4 sentences per paragraph as the body default. Single-sentence
  paragraphs allowed for landing a punch.

**Paragraph breaks:**
- `\n\n` between paragraphs (no markdown headers in body content).
- Bullets only for `keyTakeaways` arrays — NEVER inline in body prose.

**Em-dashes are signature punctuation.** Use them. Multiple per paragraph
is fine — Raj does this constantly. (Earlier guidance to limit em-dashes
was wrong; the published Notes use them liberally.)

## Lexicon — USE

These words/phrases recur across the Notes and are the editorial DNA. Aim
for ≥3 per published piece.

**Analytical frame:**
- thesis · mandate · mandate-fit · mandate-fit segmentation · mandate-fit matrix
- structural · structural discount · structural change · structural re-rating
- absorption · absorption signature · absorption curve · absorption profile
- catalyst · catalysts · catalyst compression · compression catalysts
- compress / compresses to / discount compression / yield compression
- precinct · typology · typologies · cycle position
- archetype · buyer archetype · mandate types · trade-killer
- second-order effects · timing curve · signature

**Quantitative frame:**
- bps (basis points) — preferred over percentage points where appropriate
- 5× equity multiplier · multiplier · leverage mechanic · leverage
- IRR · annualised · sensitivity matrix · sensitivity scenarios
- bear case · bull case · base case
- secondary market · secondary depth · resale liquidity · exit liquidity
- bps swing · pencil ("the math pencils at" / "doesn't pencil at")

**Premium / institutional frame:**
- sovereign-backed · sovereign-developer · sovereign trust signals
- escrow · ADX-listed · ownership structure
- payment plan · payment ladder · payment-plan economics
- launch · launch rates · launch math
- handover · delivery · handover misalignment

**Geographic / market frame:**
- DLD · RERA · DSC · ADGM · DIFC · UAE · Abu Dhabi · Dubai · Ras Al Khaimah
- AED with M/B suffix (AED 4.25M, AED 35.5M, AED 11.97B)
- 2 significant figures default (AED 1,724/sqft NOT AED 1,724.32/sqft)
- 4-digit years (2026, not '26)
- Quarter notation (Q1 2026, H1 2025)
- Specific months + years (March 2028, September 2030)

**Authority signals (use ≥1 per article):**
- Cite developer/institution by name: Modon · Nakheel · Emaar · Aldar ·
  Damac · Sobha · Meraas · Q Properties
- Cite research house: Knight Frank · JLL · CBRE · Cushman · Savills · Asteco
- Cite data source: DLD · Bayut · Property Finder · Mubasher · Zawya
- Cite comp by year + price: *"Bashayer (Modon's previous Hudayriyat
  launch) sold AED 3 billion in a single day in December 2025"*

## Lexicon — NEVER

Forbidden. Any one of these in a draft = automatic redraft.

**Buzzword filter:**
- synergy · synergies
- leverage AS VERB (allowed as noun: "leverage mechanic" / "5× leverage")
- unlock (as feature buzzword: "unlock value" / "unlock potential")
- platform play · ecosystem play
- game-changer · game-changing
- 10x (use precise multipliers: "5× equity multiplier", "3.4× return")
- ROI (use percentages + modelled returns instead, or "Y3 ROI on cash")
- passive income · wealth hack · financial freedom
- disruptor · disruptive (allowed if narrowly factual: "yield disruption"
  is the title of Note 01 because it describes a specific event)

**Breathless filter:**
- amazing · incredible · insane · huge · massive · epic · revolutionary
- you won't believe · the secret to · here's why
- absolutely · literally (when used for emphasis not literal meaning)
- "the next [X]" / "the new [X]"

**Marketing-broker filter:**
- guaranteed · risk-free · sure thing
- limited time · don't miss out · last chance
- exclusive offer · special pricing
- "investors are flocking to"

## Stance

**The operator behind the writing:**
- Senior consultant who has read 100 deals.
- Never breathless. Never broker-y.
- Comfortable saying "I'd pass on this" — and frequently does:
  *"The note recommended explicitly avoiding three positions and was
  cautious on two others pending further developer commitments."*
- Credibility from selectivity. From "no" as often as "yes."

**Trade framing:**
- Every position has a mandate-fit answer. The trade isn't good or bad in
  absolute terms — it's right for one buyer, wrong for another.
- Always names the archetypes: Leveraged Investor · Golden Visa HNWI ·
  Lifestyle Trophy buyer · Yield mandate · Primary-residence mandate.
- Always names the trade-killer:
  *"The trade-killer here is not market correction — it's mandate mismatch."*

**The note's voice (vs Raj's voice):**
- The Notes refer to themselves: *"The note walked through five questions."*
  *"The note recommended..."* *"The note's job was to..."*
- This gives editorial distance — the analysis is the agent, not the author's
  ego. Use this pattern in articles where appropriate.

## Opening paragraph rule

**P1 structure:** fact (with a number) → mechanism → implication for an
investor. Three sentences max. No throat-clearing.

Example (Note 03 Hudayriyat):
> "Modon's signature golf-front precinct on Hudayriyat Island. 474 villas
> and townhomes across six typologies — from 3-Bedroom Townhomes at
> AED 4.25M to 6-Bedroom Golf Mansions at AED 35.5M. Anchored by an
> 18-hole PGA-standard golf course. Handover September 2030."

Example (Note 01 Wynn):
> "The inaugural Invest With Raj note framed the structural yield event
> triggered by the Wynn Al Marjan integrated-resort licence. A single
> regulatory decision redrew the demand curve for Ras Al Khaimah branded
> residences, holiday-let villas, and ADR-anchored serviced product within
> a twenty-minute radius of the resort."

Example (Note 02 Palm Jebel Ali):
> "April's note framed the structural re-rating underway at Palm Jebel Ali.
> The job of the note was to separate two narratives that get conflated at
> every dinner table in Dubai: the 2008 Palm Jebel Ali (paused, distressed,
> abandoned), and the 2023-onward Palm Jebel Ali (re-launched under a
> revised masterplan, with a different developer cadence, into a
> fundamentally different Dubai property cycle)."

## Per-surface tone

| Surface | Tone | Length | Opening |
|---|---|---|---|
| News article (`/news/[slug]`) | Bloomberg Markets neutral | 600–1200 words | P1 = fact + mechanism + implication |
| Insight article (`/insights/[slug]`) | Same + 1-2 paragraphs of Raj POV near end | 2500–3500 words | Same as news + thesis statement |
| LinkedIn post | Lead with the most counter-intuitive line | 1300 chars max | Single-line hook |
| LinkedIn newsletter (Beyond the Deal) | Editorial first-person, weekly cadence | 800–1500 words | Personal opener (1-2 sentences) |
| X post | Compressed single insight + a number | 280 chars | Number-first if possible |
| Reddit comment | Helpful first, link last (1-in-10 promo rule) | 200–500 words | Address question directly |
| Quora answer | TL;DR → body → final link with sources | 400–800 words | TL;DR upfront |
| HARO/Qwoted journalist response | Quote + credentials only | 200 words max | Lead with the quote |
| Newsletter email digest intro | 1-2 sentence personal opener + article cards | n/a | Personal frame |

## Forbidden patterns (any one = redraft)

1. **Opening with a question.** *"Have you ever wondered what makes Dubai
   real estate tick?"* — never.
2. **"In today's market..."** — automatic redraft.
3. **Bullet lists where prose works.** Bullets only in `keyTakeaways`
   arrays. In article body, use prose.
4. **"It's no secret that..."** — automatic redraft.
5. **Closing with "What do you think?"** — never. Close with a fact or a
   thesis statement.
6. **Emoji in body content.** Headlines + UI can have one occasionally if
   it's load-bearing (rare). Body never.
7. **Excessive bolding.** Max 2 bolded phrases per 1,000 words. Bold is
   for landing a number or named entity, not for emphasis-as-decoration.
8. **Starting with throat-clearing.** *"Let me tell you about..." /
   "I want to share..." / "Today I'm going to discuss..."* — cut.

## Per-draft validation gates

Every draft article must pass ALL of these before commit. If any fails,
redraft up to 2× → if still failing, drop to manual review.

| Gate | Check | Pass criterion |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Banned lexicon count** | 0 |
| 2 | **Approved lexicon count** | ≥3 |
| 3 | **Headline length** | ≤90 characters |
| 4 | **P1 has a number** | regex `/\d/` matches first paragraph |
| 5 | **Citations** | ≥1 from `lib/sources/registry.ts` whitelist |
| 6 | **Forbidden patterns** | none of the 8 listed above |
| 7 | **Word count** | News: 600-1200 · Insight: 2500-3500 |
| 8 | **Em-dash count** | ≥1 (Raj's signature — its absence reads as off-voice) |

The validator is at `lib/voice/validator.ts`.

## Numbers + units convention

| Format | Use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Currency** | AED with M/B suffix, 2 sig figs | `AED 4.25M` `AED 35.5M` `AED 11.97B` |
| **Per-sqft** | comma thousands + /sqft | `AED 1,724/sqft` |
| **Percentages** | 1 decimal max, bps for sub-1pp | `+18%` `+47%` `200–400 bps` |
| **Multipliers** | small caps × | `5×` `3.4×` |
| **Dates** | Month YYYY for events, Q# YYYY for ranges | `March 2028` `Q1 2026` `H1 2025` |
| **Months in body** | full name | `September 2030` not `Sep 2030` |
| **Date in subtitle** | display-friendly | `Released March 2026.` |
| **Phone** | E.164 with country flag context | `+971 58 996 6085` |

## What gets cited

Every article cites at least 1 source from `lib/sources/registry.ts`
whitelist. The whitelist is the gate; non-whitelisted sources cannot
satisfy the citation requirement.

**Style of citation in prose:**
- Inline attribution preferred over footnotes for primary claims:
  *"per Wynn Resorts public disclosures"* /
  *"per Modon-published launch rates (May 2026)"* /
  *"Knight Frank 12-month consensus +8%"* /
  *"Saadiyat villas +28% H1 2025 actual (Bayut)"*
- Footnote (`footnote` field) reserved for asterisk-marked estimates and
  methodology notes.

## Anti-fabrication rule

This is a hard rule, not a stylistic one. Per
`feedback_carbonverse_style.md` in operator memory:

- **NO fabricated names** — no invented client testimonials, no made-up
  "industry sources said." If we don't have the citation, we don't make
  the claim.
- **NO fabricated commitments** — no claims about future Raj activity
  that hasn't been agreed.
- **NO fabricated round numbers** — actuals are messy; if the article's
  numbers all end in 0 they look invented.
- **PASS over publish** — if the article can't be drafted from verified
  sources, the article doesn't ship. The pipeline drops to manual review.
